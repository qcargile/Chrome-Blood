Player Guide

Chrome & Blood reworks most of the game. This is what you need to know to play it — the controls, the systems that changed, and what each profile does differently.

This is the how-to-play stuff. If you hit a specific problem, the FAQ covers those, and if you want to change the list yourself, read Rule 11 first. Almost everything here can be adjusted in the in-game Mod Settings.

00 · GETTING STARTED

Getting Started

  • Play on Very Hard. The list is balanced around it; the normal difficulty slider still stacks on top.
  • Your lifepath sets your starting attributes now, so pick it with the build you want in mind.
  • Dying early is mostly the point. Combat is lethal — use cover and pick your fights. If it's too rough, turn Damage Scaling down in Mod Settings.
  • Money is tight at first. You earn from jobs — gigs, contracts, and bounties — not from looting.
  • You can't quickhack on sight. Breach a network first: knock someone out and breach them, or breach an access point.
  • On Immersive, a lot of the HUD is hidden or locked behind cyberware. Install what it asks for, and toggle the minimap with F6 or F8.
  • You craft at a stash until you unlock the perk for it.
  • If fixers aren't calling, do some NCPD activity in that district first.
  • If your loot icons vanished, that's Muted Markers — turn the ones you want back on in its settings.
  • If it's too dark at night, raise Shadow Boost under ENV Tuner.

02 · COMBAT

Combat

Combat is fast and deadly. Enemies use cover, flank, and call in backup when a fight drags, and they notice you faster than they used to.

You can die in a couple of hits. So can they, so lead with cover and a good opening instead of walking in.

You've got more than your gun, too. Grab a downed body for cover, bounce shots off walls, or fall back on the quick-melee. Ammo runs out, so watch your count, and turrets hit harder than you'd expect.

Your armor takes damage as well. That blue percentage by your health is its integrity, and when it drops you get it patched at a ripperdoc, on the cyberware screen.

03 · HACKING & STEALTH

Hacking & Stealth

Hacking is rebuilt. You can't just scan someone and quickhack them anymore; you have to breach their network first.

Two ways in: knock an enemy out and breach them, or breach an access point. As your Cyberdeck and Intelligence go up, more of your quickhacks come back online.

Enemy netrunners are a real threat now. Push your luck and they'll catch on, then slow your uploads, lock networks back down, and trace where you are. Ease off, or go hunt them.

Stealth is tighter, too. Enemies pay closer attention, they react to bodies you leave lying around, and optical camo isn't the free pass it used to be. Move your kills out of sight and don't lean on the camo too hard.

04 · TIME DILATION

Time Dilation & Sandevistans

Here's the one to remember: enemies can move inside your slow-mo now. A Sandevistan doesn't freeze the world and keep you safe like it does in vanilla. Anyone with the right chrome keeps coming, so you still dodge and use cover.

After that, it runs deep. There are eleven Sandevistans and they all play nothing alike, and a few don't slow time at all. Slowing time hard wears on your body, and sitting in scanner slow-mo to line up hacks costs you now.

Try a few and run whatever suits you. The full roster's on the mod page, and you can tune all of it under Mods → TDO. The Kerenzikov picks up an air-dodge on top.

05 · MONEY & THE CITY

Money & The World

You make money from work, not loot. Gigs, contracts, and bounties pay; junk barely sells. Later fixers pay far more than the ones you start with, and taking cyberpsychos in alive beats killing them.

Want steady income? There are restored bounties and repeatable NCPD gigs for that.

Vendors warm up to you by district. Do jobs in an area and its shops give you better prices. Fixers are the same. They won't call about a district until you've done some NCPD work there.

The new weapons, clothes, and cars are all bought in-game. Gear's through Virtual Atelier on any computer's Net menu; cars are through the Virtual Car Dealer.

There's new stuff out in the world too: a few added quests, and NPCs that remember how you treated them.

06 · BUILDS & CHROME

Builds, Perks & Cyberware

Merc Protocol adds a second set of perks on top of the vanilla trees. You earn the points by working — gigs, cyberpsychos, side jobs, NCPD assaults — and spend them in extra trees you open from the Character screen, under the Additional Perk Trees button.

These aren't stat boosts. Each perk gives you something new to do, and you can't afford all of them, so you'll have to pick a direction. Vanilla perk scaling is reworked on top, and there are new sprint perks in the mix.

Cyberware carries more weight here. You've got real slot limits and a humanity cost, so you can't just bolt on everything. What you run is part of your build, and on Immersive it also controls how much HUD you get.

There's a lot of new chrome to find: full-body conversions, chipware, and a set of iconic cyberarms. The Berserk, Kerenzikov, and Pyromania cyberware all have reworked versions, and you can break down unique weapons to learn their recipes.

07 · DRIVING

Getting Around

Driving feels different. Handling's reworked on cars and bikes, so they grip and corner differently than stock; give it a minute to settle in.

There's a nitrous boost for a burst of speed (the key's in the table), and you can fire shotguns and heavy weapons from behind the wheel now, not just pistols. New rides come from the Virtual Car Dealer.

08 · STANDARD PROFILE

The Standard Profile

Standard is the full reworked game without the survival layer. You keep the whole UI from the start, fast travel and transit work like normal, and there's nothing extra to track. Everything above still applies; you just skip the friction below.

09 · IMMERSIVE PROFILE

The Immersive Profile

Immersive stacks a survival layer on top of everything else:

  • Survival needs to manage, and no fast travel (Dark Future).
  • Most of the HUD is hidden or locked behind cyberware — install the chrome, and toggle the minimap with F6/F8.
  • Reloading is manual, and a reload throws away whatever rounds were left in the magazine.
  • Fast travel costs in-game time, and public transit and taxis cost eddies.
  • Anything you buy from Virtual Atelier arrives after a delivery delay.
10 · GRAPHICS & FIXES

Graphics, Performance & Fixes

If performance or visuals need work, the FAQ's Graphics & Performance section has the full walkthrough — DLSS Swapper versions, LUT Switcher for the color grade, and the fix for night being too dark.

Stuck on something the guide doesn't cover? The FAQ handles specific problems, and the Discord is there if you're still stuck.
