Player Guide

Chrome & Blood reworks most of the game. This covers what you need to know to play it — the controls, the systems that changed, and what each profile does differently.

This is the how-to-play side. For a specific problem, the FAQ has direct answers, and for changing the list yourself there's Rule 11. Almost everything below is adjustable in the in-game Mod Settings menus.

00 · GETTING STARTED

Getting Started

  • Play on Very Hard. The list is balanced around it; the normal difficulty slider still stacks on top.
  • Your lifepath sets your starting attributes now, so pick it with the build you want in mind.
  • Dying early is mostly the point. Combat is lethal — use cover and pick your fights. If it's too rough, turn Damage Scaling down in Mod Settings.
  • Money is tight at first. You earn from jobs — gigs, contracts, and bounties — not from looting.
  • You can't quickhack on sight. Breach a network first: knock someone out and breach them, or breach an access point.
  • On Immersive, a lot of the HUD is hidden or locked behind cyberware. Install what it asks for, and toggle the minimap with F6 or F8.
  • You craft at a stash until you unlock the perk for it.
  • If fixers aren't calling, do some NCPD activity in that district first.
  • If your loot icons vanished, that's Muted Markers — turn the ones you want back on in its settings.
  • If it's too dark at night, raise Shadow Boost under ENV Tuner.

02 · COMBAT

Combat

Fights are quick and lethal for everyone. Enemies flank, take cover, fall back, and call in reinforcements, and they spot you faster than vanilla — cover and positioning matter. You'll drop in a couple of hits, and so will they.

You've got more options than just shooting: grab a downed body to use as a shield, bounce rounds off surfaces, and there's a dedicated quick-melee. Ammo is limited, so plan your resupply, and security turrets hit harder now.

Your armor has an integrity value — the blue percentage next to your health — that wears down over time. Repair it at a ripperdoc on the cyberware screen.

03 · HACKING & STEALTH

Hacking & Stealth

Hacking is rebuilt. You can't scan an enemy and quickhack them on sight — quickhacks stay locked until you breach the network. Knock someone unconscious and breach them, or breach a nearby access point. As your Cyberdeck and Intelligence climb, more of your quickhacks open back up.

Enemy netrunners roam and fight back if you lean on hacking too hard — slowing your uploads, re-locking networks, and tracing your position. Ease off, or take them out.

Stealth is tighter. Enemies are more observant, they react to bodies, and optical camo behaves more realistically than vanilla. Move bodies out of sight, and don't assume camo makes you untouchable.

04 · TIME DILATION

Time Dilation & Sandevistans

The main thing to know: enemies can move inside your time dilation now. Hitting a Sandevistan doesn't freeze the world and make you safe the way it does in vanilla — anyone with the right cyberware keeps fighting, so you still dodge and use cover.

Past that, there are eleven Sandevistan operating systems and they each play completely differently — a few don't slow time at all. Slowing time hard strains your body, and queuing quickhacks in scanner slow-mo isn't free. Pick one that fits how you fight; the full roster's on the mod's page, and it's all adjustable under Mods → TDO. The Kerenzikov gets an air-dodge on top.

05 · MONEY & THE CITY

Money & The World

Your income is work, not loot. Gigs, fixer contracts, and bounties pay; junk barely sells. Later fixers pay much more than the early ones, and bringing cyberpsychos in alive beats killing them. There are restored bounties and repeatable NCPD gigs if you want steady work.

Vendors react to your reputation per district — the more jobs you do somewhere, the better its prices get. Fixers also won't start calling about a district until you've done some NCPD activity there.

The list's added weapons, clothing, and even vehicles are bought in-game: gear through Virtual Atelier (the Net menu on any computer), and cars through the Virtual Car Dealer. There's new content woven in too — a handful of extra quests, and NPCs that remember how you've treated them.

06 · BUILDS & CHROME

Builds, Perks & Cyberware

Merc Protocol adds a second layer of perks earned from merc work — gigs, cyberpsychos, side jobs, NCPD assaults — spent in extra trees you open from the Character screen, under the Additional Perk Trees button. They give you new abilities rather than bigger numbers, and you can't buy everything, so you'll have to choose. Vanilla perk scaling is reworked on top of that, with new sprint perks in the mix.

Cyberware carries real weight: slot limits and a humanity cost mean what you install is part of your build, and on Immersive it also controls how much HUD you get. There's a lot of new chrome to find — full-body conversions, chipware, and a set of iconic cyberarms — and the Berserk, Kerenzikov, and Pyromania cyberware all have reworked versions. You can also disassemble unique weapons to learn their recipes.

07 · DRIVING

Getting Around

Handling is reworked across cars and bikes — they grip and steer differently, so give it a minute. There's a nitrous boost (the key's in the table above), and you can fire shotguns and heavy weapons from the driver's seat now, not just sidearms. New rides are bought from the Virtual Car Dealer.

08 · STANDARD PROFILE

The Standard Profile

Standard is the full reworked game without the survival layer. You keep the complete UI from the start, fast travel and transit work normally, and there's nothing extra to manage — everything in the sections above applies, just without the friction below.

09 · IMMERSIVE PROFILE

The Immersive Profile

Immersive stacks a survival layer on top of everything else:

  • Survival needs to manage, and no fast travel (Dark Future).
  • Most of the HUD is hidden or locked behind cyberware — install the chrome, and toggle the minimap with F6/F8.
  • Reloading is manual, and a reload throws away whatever rounds were left in the magazine.
  • Fast travel costs in-game time, and public transit and taxis cost eddies.
  • Anything you buy from Virtual Atelier arrives after a delivery delay.
10 · GRAPHICS & FIXES

Graphics, Performance & Fixes

If performance or visuals need work, the FAQ's Graphics & Performance section has the full setup — DLSS Swapper versions, LUT Switcher for the color grade, and the fix for night being too dark.

Stuck on something the guide doesn't cover? The FAQ handles specific problems, and the Discord is there if you're still stuck.
