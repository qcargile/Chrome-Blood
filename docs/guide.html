Player Guide

Chrome & Blood changes most of the game's systems. This covers the controls, what plays differently from vanilla, and what each profile does.

For a specific problem, check the FAQ. To change the list yourself, read Rule 11 first. Most of what's below can be adjusted in the in-game Mod Settings.

00 · GETTING STARTED

Getting Started

  • Play on Very Hard. The list is balanced around it. The vanilla difficulty slider still applies on top.
  • Your lifepath sets your starting attributes, so pick it with the build you want in mind.
  • Combat is lethal. Use cover and pick your fights. To make it easier, lower Damage Scaling in Mod Settings.
  • Money is tight at first. You earn from jobs (gigs, contracts, bounties), not from looting.
  • You can't quickhack on sight. Breach a network first: knock someone out and breach them, or breach an access point.
  • On Immersive, much of the HUD is hidden or locked behind cyberware. Install the cyberware it asks for, toggle the minimap with F6, or toggle the full HUD configuration with F8.
  • You craft at a stash until you unlock the perk for it.
  • Fixers won't call until you've done NCPD activity in their district.
  • Missing loot icons are Muted Markers. Re-enable the ones you want in its settings.
  • If nighttime is too dark, open the CET overlay and raise Shadow Boost under Nova Optics → Exposure Overrides.

02 · COMBAT

Combat

Combat is fast and lethal. Enemies use cover, flank, and call in reinforcements, and they spot you faster than in vanilla.

You can die in a few hits, so stay in cover and don't rush into the open.

You can grab a downed body for cover, ricochet shots off surfaces, and use the quick-melee. Ammo is limited, and security turrets are tougher than in vanilla.

Armor has an integrity value, shown as the blue percentage next to your health. It degrades over time, and you repair it at a ripperdoc on the cyberware screen.

03 · HACKING & STEALTH

Hacking & Stealth

Quickhacks are locked until you breach a target's network. You can't scan an enemy and hack them on sight.

There are two ways to breach: knock an enemy out and breach them, or breach an access point. Higher Cyberdeck tiers and Intelligence unlock more of your quickhacks.

Enemy netrunners react if you over-hack. They slow your uploads, re-lock networks, and trace your position. Avoid them or take them out.

Stealth is harder. Enemies are more observant, they react to bodies you leave behind, and optical camo is weaker than in vanilla. Hide your kills, and don't rely on the camo.

04 · TIME DILATION

Time Dilation & Sandevistans

Enemies can move inside your time dilation. A Sandevistan no longer freezes the world and keeps you safe. Anyone with the right cyberware keeps fighting, so you still have to dodge and use cover.

There are eleven Sandevistans, and they each play differently. A few don't slow time at all. Slowing time too hard damages your body, and scanning in slow-mo to queue hacks drains a bar.

Pick the one that fits how you play. The full list is on the mod's page, and you can adjust everything under Mods → TDO. The Kerenzikov also gets an air-dodge.

05 · MONEY & THE CITY

Money & The World

Income comes from jobs, not loot. Gigs, contracts, and bounties pay; selling junk barely does. Later fixers pay more than early ones, and capturing cyberpsychos alive pays more than killing them.

Restored bounties and repeatable NCPD gigs give you steady work, and you can order some services straight from your phone.

Vendor prices depend on your reputation in each district. Doing jobs in an area lowers its prices. Fixers also won't call about a district until you've done NCPD activity there.

The added weapons and clothing are sold through Virtual Atelier, on any computer's Net menu. Modded weapons are level-gated and have craftable schematics, and their prices scale with the economy. Added vehicles are sold through the Virtual Car Dealer.

There are also new quests, and NPCs that remember how you treated them.

06 · BUILDS & CHROME

Builds, Perks & Cyberware

Merc Protocol adds extra perk trees on top of the vanilla ones. You earn points from merc work (gigs, cyberpsychos, side jobs, NCPD assaults) and spend them from the Character screen, under the Additional Perk Trees button.

These perks give you new abilities, not stat increases. You can't afford all of them, so you'll specialize. Vanilla perk scaling is also reworked, and there are new sprint perks.

Cyberware has slot limits and a humanity cost, so you can't install everything. What you run is part of your build. On Immersive, cyberware also controls how much of your HUD is visible.

There's new cyberware throughout the list, including chipware and iconic cyberarms. The Berserk, Kerenzikov, and Pyromania cyberware have reworked versions. You can also disassemble unique weapons to learn their recipes.

07 · DRIVING

Getting Around

Vehicle handling is reworked, so cars and bikes grip and steer differently than in vanilla.

There's a nitrous boost for extra speed (key in the table above). You can fire shotguns and heavy weapons from vehicles now, not just sidearms. Added vehicles are bought from the Virtual Car Dealer.

08 · STANDARD PROFILE

The Standard Profile

Standard is the full reworked game without the survival layer. You have the complete UI from the start, fast travel and transit work normally, and there are no needs to manage. Everything above still applies.

09 · IMMERSIVE PROFILE

The Immersive Profile

Immersive adds a survival layer on top of everything else:

  • Survival needs to manage, and no fast travel (Dark Future).
  • Much of the HUD is hidden or locked behind cyberware. Install the cyberware, toggle the minimap with F6, or toggle the full HUD configuration with F8.
  • Reloading is manual, and a reload discards the rounds left in the magazine.
  • Fast travel costs in-game time. Public transit and taxis cost eddies.
  • Purchases from Virtual Atelier arrive after a delay.
